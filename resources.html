Trauma & Survival Response Resources

Understanding trauma and survival responses is foundational to WRH. These resources provide additional context and information:

Polyvagal Theory

Understanding how the nervous system responds to threat and safety. Foundational for understanding freeze responses and survival wiring.

Key Concepts: Vagal tone, parasympathetic activation, social engagement system

Trauma-Informed Care

Principles and practices for working with trauma-affected individuals in ways that promote safety, trustworthiness, and empowerment.

Key Concepts: Safety, trustworthiness, peer support, collaboration, empowerment

Complex PTSD (C-PTSD)

Understanding complex trauma resulting from prolonged exposure to traumatic events, particularly in institutional or relational contexts.

Key Concepts: Affect dysregulation, negative self-perception, relational difficulties

Crisis & Safety Resources

If you are in crisis or experiencing thoughts of self-harm, please reach out for help immediately:

988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline

Call or text 988 (available 24/7)

Free, confidential support for people in suicidal crisis or emotional distress.

Crisis Text Line

Text HOME to 741741

Free, confidential text-based crisis support available 24/7.

National Domestic Violence Hotline

1-800-799-7233 or text START to 88788

Support for individuals experiencing domestic violence.

SAMHSA National Helpline

1-800-662-4357 (free, confidential, 24/7)

Information and referral for substance abuse and mental health services.

Mental Health & Clinical Services

WRH is educational and does not replace clinical mental health services. If you need professional support, consider:

  • Therapists/Counselors: Individual or group therapy for trauma, mental health conditions, or behavioral health
  • Psychiatrists: Medical evaluation and medication management
  • Community Mental Health Centers: Comprehensive services often available on a sliding fee scale
  • Peer Support Groups: Support from others with lived experience
  • Substance Abuse Treatment: Specialized services for substance use disorders

Resilience & Wellness Resources

Resources for building resilience and supporting overall wellness:

Mindfulness & Meditation

Practices for calming the nervous system, building present-moment awareness, and reducing stress.

Resources: Insight Timer, Calm, Headspace (some free options available)

Physical Activity & Exercise

Movement and exercise as tools for nervous system regulation and overall health.

Resources: Community recreation centers, free online fitness videos, walking groups

Creative Expression

Art, music, writing, and other creative practices for processing experiences and building resilience.

Resources: Community arts programs, writing groups, music therapy

Community Connection

Building supportive relationships and community engagement as protective factors.

Resources: Peer support groups, community organizations, volunteer opportunities

Educational Resources

Additional learning resources on trauma, resilience, and related topics:

  • Books: "The Body Keeps the Score" by Bessel van der Kolk, "What Happened to You?" by Bruce Perry and Oprah Winfrey
  • Online Courses: Free and low-cost courses on trauma, resilience, and mental health
  • Podcasts: Interviews and discussions on trauma, recovery, and resilience
  • Videos: Educational videos on nervous system regulation, trauma responses, and coping strategies
