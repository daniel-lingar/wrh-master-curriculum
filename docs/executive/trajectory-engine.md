# Trajectory Engine

## Dark Mode Executive Edition

The Trajectory Engine is WRH’s directional-change model.

It explains how a person moves from automatic survival pattern to repeated, measurable change.

Most systems focus on behavior after it appears.

WRH focuses on the sequence that produces the behavior.

The Trajectory Engine gives staff, participants, agencies, and evaluators a shared model for identifying where change can actually occur.

The sequence is:

**Pattern → Fork → Interrupt → Repetition → Evidence → Direction**

This is the core WRH model for change.

---

## 1. Pattern

A pattern is the repeated survival sequence a person’s system has learned to run.

It may look like:

- relapse
- shutdown
- avoidance
- anger
- disappearing
- missed appointments
- conflict
- self-sabotage
- emotional flooding
- overfunctioning
- collapse

WRH does not treat the visible behavior as the whole story.

The behavior is the end of the sequence.

The pattern began earlier.

The first task is to make the pattern visible.

---

## 2. Fork

The fork is the moment where direction can change.

It is the point between automatic activation and automatic action.

The fork may be small.

It may last only seconds.

But it matters because this is where interruption becomes possible.

WRH teaches participants and staff to look for the fork before the pattern completes itself.

Without the fork, systems only respond after the damage is already done.

With the fork, direction becomes possible.

---

## 3. Interrupt

An interrupt is a small action that breaks the automatic sequence.

It is not a total life transformation.

It is not a perfect response.

It is not proof that the person is fixed.

An interrupt may be:

- pausing
- naming the Alarm
- leaving before escalation
- asking for help
- using a safer replacement
- showing up late instead of disappearing
- sending a message instead of shutting down
- choosing one stabilizing action before the pattern completes

The interrupt matters because it creates a new data point.

The goal is not perfection.

The goal is evidence.

---

## 4. Repetition

One interrupt does not change a trajectory.

Repetition does.

The nervous system updates through repeated evidence that a new direction is possible.

WRH emphasizes repetition because insight alone is not enough.

A participant can understand the pattern and still return to it.

Repetition turns understanding into movement.

Each repeated interrupt weakens the old automatic sequence and strengthens the new direction.

---

## 5. Evidence

Evidence is what the system collects when a new action is repeated.

Evidence tells the nervous system:

- this did not destroy me
- this did not make me unsafe
- this did not collapse everything
- this created a different outcome
- this direction may be possible again

Evidence is not a motivational slogan.

It is lived proof.

WRH tracks evidence because change must become visible, measurable, and repeatable.

---

## 6. Direction

Direction is the stabilized movement that forms after repeated interrupts build enough evidence.

Direction does not mean the person never struggles.

It means the pattern is no longer the only available route.

Direction may look like:

- increased retention
- reduced crisis use
- fewer relapses
- fewer missed appointments
- improved communication
- safer support use
- increased stability
- improved follow-through
- greater staff confidence
- measurable participant movement

Direction is the outcome WRH is built to support.

---

## Why the Trajectory Engine Matters

The Trajectory Engine gives agencies a way to measure movement before final outcomes appear.

Many systems only count success at the end:

- completion
- abstinence
- employment
- discharge
- no re-arrest
- no crisis event

Those outcomes matter, but they come late.

WRH gives systems earlier indicators:

- pattern visibility
- fork recognition
- interrupt attempts
- repeated safer actions
- evidence accumulation
- directional stability

This allows agencies to see change while it is forming.

---

## Relationship to the Survival Map

The Survival Map explains where the pattern came from.

The Trajectory Engine explains how direction changes.

Together, they create the WRH core:

- understand the pattern
- find the fork
- interrupt the sequence
- repeat the safer action
- build evidence
- stabilize direction

This is the movement from survival pattern to directional change.

---

## Summary Line

The Trajectory Engine shows how change happens: not through punishment, pressure, or willpower, but through repeated interrupts that build evidence and create direction.
