# Deployment System

## Dark Mode Executive Edition

The WRH Deployment System is the layer that ensures WRH is delivered consistently, predictably, and with fidelity across programs, staff, shifts, and sites.

If the Program System explains what WRH teaches, the Deployment System explains:

> How do we actually run this inside a real agency?

It includes:

- Deployment Checklist
- Implementation Roadmap
- Fidelity Tools
- Participant Materials

This is the operational backbone of WRH.

---

## 1. Deployment Checklist

The Deployment Checklist outlines everything an agency needs to launch WRH successfully.

It includes:

- staff training schedule
- workshop schedule
- coaching cadence
- reporting setup
- fidelity expectations
- evaluation alignment
- participant flow
- documentation requirements

This ensures nothing is missed during startup.

---

## 2. Implementation Roadmap

The Implementation Roadmap provides a month-by-month rollout plan, including:

- training
- workshops
- coaching
- fidelity checks
- evaluation milestones
- reporting deadlines

The roadmap gives agencies a predictable structure and prevents drift.

It also helps leadership understand:

- what happens first
- what happens next
- what happens every month
- what outcomes to expect

---

## 3. Fidelity Tools

Fidelity Tools ensure WRH is delivered the way it was designed.

They include:

- facilitator checklists
- session rubrics
- coaching notes
- observation tools
- fidelity snapshots
- alignment guides

Fidelity Tools protect:

- program quality
- participant experience
- outcome reliability
- evaluation accuracy

Without fidelity, WRH becomes a training.

With fidelity, WRH becomes a system.

---

## 4. Participant Materials

Participant Materials include:

- handouts
- worksheets
- pattern maps
- interrupt practice tools
- directional tracking sheets

These materials:

- reinforce the Survival Map
- reinforce the Trajectory Engine
- support interrupt practice
- support evidence building
- support directional stability

They are intentionally:

- non-clinical
- non-exposure-based
- non-confessional
- non-punitive

They give participants a mechanical, not emotional, way to understand and change patterns.

---

## Why the Deployment System Matters

The Deployment System:

- ensures consistency
- ensures fidelity
- ensures predictability
- ensures measurable outcomes
- ensures staff confidence
- ensures participant stability
- ensures agency alignment

It is the layer that turns WRH from a training into a fully operational program.

---

## Summary Line

The WRH Deployment System ensures WRH is delivered consistently, predictably, and with fidelity so agencies can achieve measurable, stable, repeatable outcomes.
