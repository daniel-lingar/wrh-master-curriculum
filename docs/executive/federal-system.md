# Federal System

## Dark Mode Executive Edition

The WRH Federal System is the layer that translates WRH into public-sector, grant, agency, and federal-review language.

It explains how WRH fits into funding environments, agency priorities, evaluation expectations, and compliance-facing systems.

This module is not the curriculum.

It is not the buyer packet.

It is not the deployment plan.

It is the federal-facing architecture of WRH.

The Federal System helps agencies and funders understand:

- the problem WRH addresses
- the structural gap WRH fills
- the population WRH serves
- the outcomes WRH supports
- the way WRH can be measured
- the way WRH can be sustained

The Federal System includes:

- Federal Abstract
- Needs Statement
- Project Description
- Goals & Objectives
- Logic Model
- Work Plan / Timeline
- Budget Justification Narrative
- Performance Measurement Plan
- Evaluation Plan
- Organizational Capacity Statement
- Sustainability Plan
- SAMHSA Compliance Language
- Letters of Support Templates

Each component supports a different part of public-sector review.

---

## 1. Federal Abstract

The Federal Abstract gives reviewers a concise summary of WRH.

It explains:

- what WRH is
- who WRH serves
- why the project matters
- what outcomes are expected
- how the project aligns with public-sector priorities

The abstract must be clear, direct, and non-clinical.

Its purpose is to help reviewers understand the full project quickly.

---

## 2. Needs Statement

The Needs Statement explains the structural problem WRH addresses.

It focuses on the gap between:

- what systems currently measure
- what participants actually experience
- where behavior appears
- where patterns begin
- how change becomes possible

The Needs Statement does not blame participants.

It does not blame agencies.

It identifies a system gap:

Most systems intervene at the visible behavior stage, after the survival sequence has already completed.

WRH addresses the pattern before the pattern becomes the outcome.

---

## 3. Project Description

The Project Description explains how WRH works inside an agency or program.

It describes:

- staff training
- participant workshops
- implementation support
- evaluation tools
- reporting systems
- sustainability planning

The Project Description turns WRH from an idea into an operational model.

It answers:

> What will actually happen if this project is funded?

---

## 4. Goals & Objectives

Goals and Objectives translate WRH into measurable commitments.

Goals define the broad direction.

Objectives define what will be done, by when, and how progress will be measured.

WRH goals may include:

- improving participant retention
- increasing pattern visibility
- increasing interrupt attempts
- reducing crisis utilization
- strengthening staff capacity
- improving program consistency
- building measurable directional outcomes

Objectives must be specific enough for evaluation and reporting.

---

## 5. Logic Model

The Logic Model shows how WRH moves from inputs to outcomes.

It connects:

- inputs
- activities
- outputs
- short-term outcomes
- intermediate outcomes
- long-term outcomes

A WRH logic model may include:

**Inputs**

- WRH framework
- trained staff
- partner agencies
- facilitator materials
- participant materials
- evaluation tools

**Activities**

- staff training
- participant workshops
- coaching
- fidelity monitoring
- data collection
- reporting

**Outputs**

- staff trained
- sessions delivered
- participants served
- reports completed
- fidelity checks conducted

**Outcomes**

- increased pattern visibility
- increased interrupt frequency
- improved retention
- reduced crisis utilization
- increased directional stability

The Logic Model shows why WRH is fundable: it connects the model to measurable change.

---

## 6. Work Plan / Timeline

The Work Plan explains when implementation steps happen.

It may include:

- planning
- onboarding
- staff training
- participant delivery
- coaching
- data collection
- reporting
- evaluation
- sustainability planning

A typical WRH implementation can be structured across:

- 30 days
- 90 days
- 6 months
- 12 months

The timeline should be simple enough for reviewers to follow and specific enough for agencies to execute.

---

## 7. Budget Justification Narrative

The Budget Justification Narrative explains why each cost is necessary.

It may include:

- personnel
- facilitator time
- training materials
- evaluation support
- reporting tools
- technical assistance
- participant materials
- administrative support
- licensing
- sustainability planning

The budget narrative should connect every cost to a project function.

The purpose is not to inflate the project.

The purpose is to show reviewers that WRH has a realistic implementation structure.

---

## 8. Performance Measurement Plan

The Performance Measurement Plan explains what WRH tracks.

WRH measurement may include:

- participant attendance
- staff training completion
- interrupt frequency
- pattern visibility
- evidence accumulation
- directional stability
- crisis utilization
- retention
- relapse/recidivism indicators
- staff confidence
- fidelity markers

This plan gives agencies a way to measure movement before final outcomes appear.

It helps reviewers see that WRH is not only teachable.

It is trackable.

---

## 9. Evaluation Plan

The Evaluation Plan explains how WRH outcomes will be reviewed.

It may include:

- process evaluation
- outcome evaluation
- fidelity review
- participant feedback
- staff feedback
- pre/post measures
- monthly reporting
- quarterly summaries
- final evaluation report

The evaluation plan should answer:

- Was WRH delivered as intended?
- Did staff use the model?
- Did participants understand the model?
- Did interrupt attempts increase?
- Did stability indicators improve?
- Did crisis use decrease?
- Did the agency gain usable data?

Evaluation turns WRH into a measurable system.

---

## 10. Organizational Capacity Statement

The Organizational Capacity Statement explains why the applicant or partner can execute WRH.

It may describe:

- relevant experience
- staff roles
- operational readiness
- community partnerships
- data capacity
- training capacity
- implementation support
- leadership structure
- past performance

This section builds reviewer confidence.

It shows that WRH can be delivered with structure, not just intention.

---

## 11. Sustainability Plan

The Sustainability Plan explains how WRH continues after initial funding.

Sustainability may include:

- internal staff training
- train-the-trainer pathways
- licensing structure
- agency budget integration
- partner support
- annual updates
- recurring implementation cycles
- outcome reporting
- future grant alignment

Sustainability matters because federal reviewers do not want temporary activity.

They want durable capacity.

WRH is designed to become part of an agency’s operating language.

---

## 12. SAMHSA Compliance Language

SAMHSA-facing language must remain clear, non-clinical, non-diagnostic, and trauma-informed.

WRH language should emphasize:

- education
- prevention
- early intervention
- resilience
- recovery support
- peer-friendly delivery
- workforce capacity
- evidence-informed structure
- measurement
- sustainability
- accessibility

WRH should not be framed as therapy, diagnosis, medical care, or clinical treatment.

It is a trauma-literacy and directional-change model that supports existing systems.

---

These letters strengthen funding applications and agency confidence.

## Why the Federal System Matters

The Federal System makes WRH understandable to reviewers, agencies, and public-sector partners.

It turns WRH into:

- a fundable project
- a measurable model
- a review-ready package
- an implementation-ready system
- a sustainability-ready framework

It protects WRH from being misunderstood as only a curriculum or only a training.

The Federal System shows that WRH has the structure needed for public-sector adoption.

The WRH Federal System translates WRH into grant-ready, agency-ready, and review-ready language so public systems can understand, fund, measure, and sustain the model.
