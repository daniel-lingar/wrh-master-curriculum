# Buyer-Facing System

## Dark Mode Executive Edition

The WRH Buyer-Facing System is the layer that helps agencies understand:

- what WRH is
- what they can purchase
- how it is delivered
- what outcomes it produces
- how it integrates into their system

This is the procurement-facing and decision-maker-facing layer of WRH.

It is not curriculum.
It is not federal content.
It is not implementation detail.

It is the business-side explanation of the WRH product.

The Buyer-Facing System includes:

- One-Page Sell Sheet
- Product Catalog
- Training Catalog
- Pilot Program Sheet
- Pricing Sheet
- Statement of Work
- License Terms
- Evaluation Framework
- Reporting Tools
- Session 16 Sample Packet

Each component serves a different purpose in the buyer journey.

---

## 1. One-Page Sell Sheet

The Sell Sheet gives directors and decision-makers a fast, high-authority overview of WRH.

It answers:

- What is WRH?
- Who is it for?
- What problem does it solve?
- What outcomes does it produce?
- What does an agency receive?

This is the first attachment in the WRH outreach sequence.

---

## 2. Product Catalog

The Product Catalog lists everything an agency can purchase, including:

- staff training
- participant workshops
- implementation support
- evaluation tools
- licensing options
- deployment packages

It is the menu of WRH offerings.

---

## 3. Training Catalog

The Training Catalog details all WRH staff-facing training modules.

It includes:

- training descriptions
- learning objectives
- delivery format
- duration
- audience
- prerequisites

This helps agencies understand how staff will be trained and what competencies they will gain.

---

## 4. Pilot Program Sheet

The Pilot Program Sheet outlines the 90-day WRH pilot, including:

- scope
- deliverables
- training
- workshops
- coaching
- evaluation
- reporting

This is the entry point for agencies that want to test WRH before full adoption.

---

## 5. Pricing Sheet

The Pricing Sheet provides:

- transparent pricing
- tiered options
- per-site or per-program models
- licensing structure
- optional add-ons

This allows agencies to understand cost without needing a full proposal.

---

## 6. Statement of Work (SOW)

The SOW is the contract-ready description of:

- scope
- deliverables
- timelines
- responsibilities
- reporting
- evaluation
- licensing terms

This is the document procurement officers use to finalize agreements.

---

## 7. License Terms

License Terms protect the WRH intellectual property and define:

- usage rights
- distribution limits
- modification restrictions
- renewal terms
- agency responsibilities

This ensures WRH is used correctly and consistently.

---

## 8. Evaluation Framework

The Evaluation Framework explains:

- what WRH measures
- how movement is tracked
- what indicators matter
- how data is collected
- how outcomes are reported

This is essential for agencies, funders, and evaluators.

---

## 9. Reporting Tools

Reporting Tools include:

- monthly reports
- quarterly summaries
- annual outcomes
- fidelity snapshots
- participant movement indicators

These tools help agencies see progress in real time.

---

## 10. Session 16 Sample Packet

The Session 16 Sample Packet is proof of product.

It shows:

- curriculum structure
- participant materials
- facilitator guidance
- fidelity alignment
- WRH voice and design

This is often the moment when buyers say:

> Oh — this is real.

---

## Why the Buyer-Facing System Matters

The Buyer-Facing System:

- reduces confusion
- accelerates procurement
- clarifies deliverables
- aligns expectations
- supports funding requests
- strengthens proposals
- demonstrates readiness
- builds trust with agencies

It is the business architecture of WRH.

---

## Summary Line

The WRH Buyer-Facing System gives agencies a clear, structured understanding of what WRH is, what they can purchase, and how the model delivers measurable outcomes.
