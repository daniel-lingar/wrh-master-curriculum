# Legacy 11-Lecture Public Education Sequence

Source: harvested from `Mave9055/cptsd-education-portal` README during WRH consolidation.

Purpose: preserve the useful public-education lecture map from the earlier CPTSD education portal so it can inform WRH public talks, short-form education, outreach pages, and lecture-based adaptations.

## Use Boundary

This sequence is not the master 75-session curriculum.

It is a legacy public-education lecture structure that may be useful for:

- public talks
- website organization
- introductory education portals
- lecture series planning
- workshop outlines
- companion content for readers
- shorter non-clinical education formats

## Legacy Lecture Sequence

1. **The Truth Behind the Silence**  
   Attachment trauma and the blueprint of survival.

2. **The Masks We Wear**  
   Fawning, perfectionism, overfunctioning, and the competence trap.

3. **When Love Hurts**  
   Trauma bonds, love bombing, and emotional repetition.

4. **The War on Reality**  
   Gaslighting, blame shifting, and reality distortion.

5. **Relapse Isn't a Betrayal**  
   Understanding addiction as a trauma-shaped survival response.

6. **What Healing Actually Looks Like**  
   Moving from survival patterns toward safer regulation, boundaries, and support.

7. **The Breaking Point**  
   Grief, guilt, loss, and collapse points.

8. **Rewriting the Legacy**  
   Family patterns, fathers, inheritance, and generational repetition.

9. **Shame vs. Responsibility**  
   Separating accountability from identity-level self-condemnation.

10. **The Lectures They Never Gave Us**  
   Trauma literacy, education gaps, and the language people were never taught.

11. **Decoding the Damage, Rewriting the Truth**  
   Science-based recovery framing and narrative repair.

## What To Preserve

The useful asset here is the **public education sequence**, not necessarily the old portal implementation.

Preserve:

- lecture titles
- progression from survival blueprint to repair
- accessible public-facing language
- lecture/discussion/facilitator-note concept
- portal-style entry path for readers or first-time learners

Do not preserve automatically:

- duplicate app scaffolding
- old branding that competes with WRH
- claims that sound clinical or treatment-based
- any wording that weakens the current non-clinical scope boundary

## Design Principle Worth Keeping

A public education sequence should let people enter through recognition before asking for commitment.

The lecture format is useful because it gives guarded readers and first-time audiences a lower-pressure way to learn the map.
