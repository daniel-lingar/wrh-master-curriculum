About the WRH Master Curriculum

Understanding the foundation, mission, and approach of the What Really Happened™ Master Curriculum.

Our Mission

The What Really Happened™ Master Curriculum is dedicated to providing comprehensive, non-clinical psychoeducation focused on survival literacy. Our mission is to empower individuals by helping them understand trauma responses, addiction patterns, freeze states, shame cycles, and survival behaviors as predictable, adaptive mechanisms rather than personal failings. We aim to foster resilience, autonomy, and effective navigation of complex systems for individuals in high-barrier environments.

Our Approach

Our curriculum is built on a foundation of neuroscience, trauma research, and behavioral adaptation theory. We utilize a structured, evidence-informed approach that prioritizes safety, non-disclosure, and measurable outcomes. Key aspects of our approach include:

  • Non-Clinical Education: We provide educational frameworks and tools, distinct from therapy or medical advice, focusing on understanding and self-regulation.
  • Safety Protocols: Robust safety guidelines and crisis management procedures are integrated throughout the curriculum to ensure a secure learning environment.
  • No Disclosure Required: Participants are not required to disclose personal trauma, fostering a safe space for learning and self-reflection.
  • Measurable Impact: Pilot programs incorporate metrics tracking and data-driven improvements to ensure effectiveness and continuous refinement.
  • Holistic Understanding: We integrate insights from various disciplines to offer a comprehensive view of human behavior in response to adversity.

Our Vision

We envision a world where individuals are equipped with the knowledge and tools to understand their own survival responses, navigate challenging environments, and build sustainable resilience. By demystifying complex psychological and behavioral patterns, we aim to reduce stigma and promote a more compassionate, informed approach to human experience.

Contact & Collaboration

We are always open to collaboration and inquiries. If you are interested in partnering, implementing the curriculum in your organization, or have any questions, please contact us.
