# Handout — Session 06
## Attachment Broke Before You Had Words

**Big idea:**  
The way you connect to people today was shaped by your very first relationships — before you could speak, before you could choose, before you could understand why closeness felt safe or terrifying.

**Why this matters**  
- If your caregivers were consistent and warm, you likely learned that people are safe.  
- If they were unpredictable, distant, or frightening, you learned that closeness is dangerous.  
- Those early lessons become automatic patterns in adult relationships — wanting love but fearing it, clinging then pushing away, feeling numb or flooded.

**What it can look like**  
- Needing constant reassurance that someone is not going to leave.  
- Pushing people away when they get too close.  
- Staying in bad relationships because being alone feels like death.  
- Feeling nothing when someone says “I love you.”

**Remember**  
Your attachment style is not a life sentence. You can learn new patterns — one small interaction at a time.

---

### Tool for Session 06: The Attachment Pause

When you notice a strong reaction in a relationship, ask yourself:

1. **What is my old attachment pattern wanting me to do right now?** (Cling? Run? Shut down? Perform?)  
2. **Is that response appropriate to this situation, or is it a replay of something much older?**  
3. **What is one small, different choice I can make right now — not perfect, just different?**

**Key sentence to carry this week:**  
> *“The way I attach was learned. The way I attach can be relearned.”*
