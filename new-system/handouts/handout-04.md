# Handout — Session 04
## Grief Does Not Leave Just Because Time Passed

**Big idea:**  
Grief is not a line you finish. It is a spiral. You will pass the same loss again and again, each time with different eyes. And if the loss was sudden, violent, or complicated, your nervous system may hold onto it for years.

**Why this matters**  
- Time does not heal wounds by itself. Safety, support, and acknowledgment do.  
- Complicated grief happens when you were not allowed to grieve, or when the loss came with trauma, guilt, or multiple deaths in a short time.  
- Unprocessed grief often shows up as anger, numbness, workaholism, addiction, or physical pain.

**What it can look like**  
- Crying unexpectedly at a song, a smell, or a date on the calendar.  
- Working constantly so you do not have to feel.  
- Feeling flat or numb when you know you should feel something.

**Remember**  
You do not have to “get over” anyone. You just have to learn to carry the grief without letting it destroy you.

---

### Tool for Session 04: The Grief Inventory (private)

Ask yourself:

1. **Who or what have I lost that I never fully grieved?** (A person, a relationship, a version of myself.)  
2. **Where does that grief show up in my life now?** (Anger? Numbness? Work? Substances?)  
3. **What is one small action I can take this week to acknowledge that grief?** (A name spoken aloud. A candle. A song. A letter you never send.)

**Key sentence to carry this week:**  
> *“Grief does not leave just because time passed. But I can learn to carry it differently.”*
