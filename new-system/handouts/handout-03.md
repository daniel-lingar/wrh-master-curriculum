# Handout — Session 03
## Unsafe Started Feeling Like Home

**Big idea:**  
Your nervous system does not chase what is good for you. It chases what is familiar. If you grew up in chaos, chaos can start feeling normal — and peace can feel suspicious or even dangerous.

**Why this matters**  
- Familiar is not the same as safe.  
- You may stay in toxic jobs, chaotic relationships, or harmful patterns because your body knows the script.  
- Calm can feel like the silence before an explosion, so your system stays on guard.

**What it can look like**  
- Feeling bored or uneasy with a steady, kind partner.  
- Thriving in high-pressure, high-criticism workplaces.  
- Creating conflict or catastrophizing when life gets too quiet.

**Remember**  
You are not broken for finding chaos familiar. You just need to teach your body that safe is not a trap — one small moment at a time.

---

### Tool for Session 03: The Familiarity Check

Ask yourself:

1. **Is this environment safe, or is it just familiar?**  
2. **If I felt genuinely safe for one hour, what would that feel like in my body?** (Tight? Suspicious? Relaxed?)  
3. **What is one small choice I can make this week to expose my nervous system to something calmer — even for five minutes?**

**Key sentence to carry this week:**  
> *“I can teach my body what safe feels like. One moment at a time.”*
