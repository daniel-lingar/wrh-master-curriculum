# Handout — Session 11
## The System Can Become the Trauma

**Big idea:**  
When courts, schools, treatment programs, or employers misread your freeze or dissociation as defiance or noncompliance, they can hurt you without ever laying a hand on you. That second wound is real.

**Why this matters**  
- Systems assume you have a choice. Often, you do not — your nervous system has locked up.  
- When they punish you for freeze, you get worse, not better.  
- Knowing this does not erase consequences, but it helps you stop believing the label.

**What it can look like**  
- Being called noncompliant for missing a court date when your body would not move.  
- Being labeled resistant in treatment when you were dissociating.  
- Being written up at work for “attitude” when you were in freeze and could not speak.

**Remember**  
Their label is not your whole truth. You are not defiant. Your body locked up. Now you need supports, not shame.

---

### Tool for Session 11: The Reframe Statement

Use this for yourself (or with an advocate):

1. **Name the behavior:** “I was told I was noncompliant when I missed ____________.”  
2. **Name the mechanism:** “What was actually happening was freeze. I was not refusing. I could not move / speak / follow through.”  
3. **Name what you need:** “I need ____________ — not just punishment.” (Reminders, written instructions, a support person, lower pressure, more time.)

**Key sentence to carry this week:**  
> *“They called it noncompliance. I call it freeze. Now I know what I need.”*
