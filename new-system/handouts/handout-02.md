# Handout — Session 02
## The Pain Did Not Start With You

**Big idea:**  
Some of what you carry — the fear, the shame, the way you react under pressure — may have started long before you were born. Pain that is never processed gets handed down through family weather, not through anyone’s fault.

**Why this matters**  
- A grandparent’s untreated trauma can shape a parent’s nervous system, which shapes the home you grew up in.  
- You did not choose that environment, but you learned to survive inside it.  
- Naming this does not blame your family — it frees you from thinking the problem is only you.

**What it can look like**  
- Noticing that your reactions look a lot like your parent’s or grandparent’s reactions.  
- Realizing that your family had “rules” no one ever said out loud: don’t cry, don’t leave, don’t make waves, be useful.  
- Carrying shame for things that were never yours to carry.

**Remember**  
The pain did not start with you. But the interruption — the choice to see the pattern and do something different — can start with you.

---

### Tool for Session 02: The Legacy Pause

Ask yourself:

1. **Is this reaction mine, or did I learn it before I had a choice?**  
2. **If this pattern started before me, does that mean I have to keep running it forever?**  
3. **What is one tiny choice I can make this week that is mine — not my family’s, not my history’s — just mine?**

**Key sentence to carry this week:**  
> *“The pain did not start with me. The interruption can.”*
