# Handout — Session 13
## They Called It Noncompliance Because They Could Not See Freeze

**Big idea:**  
Sometimes you were punished for “noncompliance” when you weren’t refusing at all — your body was frozen. Knowing this does not erase consequences, but it changes how you see yourself.

**Why this matters**  
- Noncompliance is a choice: you understand and can comply but choose not to.  
- Freeze is not a choice: your nervous system locks up; you may want to comply but your body won’t move.  
- Systems often assume behavior = choice, so they punish freeze. That punishment makes freeze worse.

**What it can look like**  
- Missing court dates, appointments, or deadlines because your body would not move.  
- Going silent in a treatment group and being labeled resistant.  
- Staring at a task you need to do, unable to start.

**Remember**  
Refusal says “I will not.” Freeze says “I cannot.” There is a difference, and it matters.

---

### Tool for Session 13: The Reframe Statement

Use this for yourself (or with an advocate):

1. **Name the behavior:** “I was told I was noncompliant when I missed ____________.”  
2. **Name the mechanism:** “What was actually happening was freeze. I was not refusing. I could not move / speak / follow through.”  
3. **Name what you need:** “I need ____________ — not just punishment.” (Reminders, written instructions, a support person, lower pressure, more time.)

**Key sentence to carry this week:**  
> *“They called it noncompliance. I call it freeze. Now I know what I need.”*
