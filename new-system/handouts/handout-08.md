# Handout — Session 08
## Your Brain Never Stopped Scanning for Danger

**Big idea:**  
Hypervigilance is not paranoia. It is a nervous system that learned to constantly scan for threats because danger used to come without warning. That scanning kept you alive. Now it is exhausting you.

**Why this matters**  
- You notice every face, every tone shift, every exit, every silence — whether you want to or not.  
- This is not a superpower. It is a survival adaptation that burns energy all day long.  
- The war may be over, but your radar is still running.

**What it can look like**  
- Being exhausted by noon even if nothing “happened.”  
- Reading people’s moods instantly and adjusting your behavior.  
- Having trouble relaxing because the moment you let your guard down, something bad might happen.

**Remember**  
You can teach your body that most rooms are not the room you grew up in — one breath at a time.

---

### Tool for Session 08: The Scan Check

When you notice tension or exhaustion, ask:

1. **What is my body scanning for right now?** (A sound? A face? A tone? A change in the room?)  
2. **Is there actual danger in this moment, or is my old radar running a drill?**  
3. **Can I take ten seconds to breathe and look at one neutral object — just to give my radar a micro-break?**

**Key sentence to carry this week:**  
> *“Is there actual danger right now, or is my old radar running a drill?”*
