# Handout — Session 07
## You Learned to Survive by Becoming Useful

**Big idea:**  
People-pleasing is not just being nice. It is often a survival response called “fawning” — learning to agree, apologize, and erase yourself to keep the peace or avoid danger.

**Why this matters**  
- If you grew up in a home where disagreement led to yelling, withdrawal, or punishment, you learned that compliance = safety.  
- Fawning may have kept you alive. But as an adult, it can cost you your voice, your boundaries, and your sense of self.

**What it can look like**  
- Saying yes when you mean no.  
- Apologizing for things that are not your fault.  
- Not knowing what you want because you are used to wanting what others want.  
- Feeling exhausted and resentful but unable to stop helping.

**Remember**  
You can be kind without erasing yourself. Your needs are not dangerous.

---

### Tool for Session 07: The Fawn Pause

When you feel the automatic “yes” rising, ask yourself:

1. **Do I actually want to say yes, or am I afraid of what will happen if I say no?**  
2. **If I said no, what is the worst that would realistically happen?** (Not the catastrophic fear — the likely outcome.)  
3. **Can I wait two seconds before I answer?** Two seconds for the automatic reflex to pass.

**Key sentence to carry this week:**  
> *“I do not have to disappear to be safe. I can say yes when I mean yes and no when I mean no.”*
