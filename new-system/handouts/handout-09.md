# Handout — Session 09
## Freeze Is Not Refusal

**Big idea:**  
When your body freezes, it is not because you are lazy, defiant, or weak. It is because your nervous system has locked up — the same way it learned to do when danger felt inescapable.

**Why this matters**  
- Freeze is a survival reflex, not a choice.  
- It can look like noncompliance, but inside it feels like being trapped behind glass.  
- Punishing freeze makes it worse. Understanding it allows you to build supports.

**What it can look like**  
- Missing court dates, appointments, or phone calls even though you know the consequences.  
- Staring at a task you need to do, unable to start.  
- Going blank when someone asks you a question or puts you on the spot.

**Remember**  
Refusal says “I will not.” Freeze says “I cannot.” There is a difference, and it matters.

---

### Tool for Session 09: Freeze First Aid

When you feel yourself locking up:

1. **Name it:** “This is freeze. My body is hitting the brakes. This is not laziness.”  
2. **Reduce the demand:** Do not try to do the whole thing. Just do one microscopic step — pick up the phone without dialing, write one word, put on your shoes.  
3. **Add support if possible:** Have someone sit with you. Or put a hand on your chest and breathe.

**Key sentence to carry this week:**  
> *“Freeze is not refusal. My body is trying to protect me. Now I can learn to work with it.”*
