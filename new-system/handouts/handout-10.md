# Handout — Session 10
## Leaving Your Body Was the Only Exit Left

**Big idea:**  
Dissociation is not weird or broken. It is your mind’s way of leaving when your body cannot. If you could not escape what was happening, your mind learned to escape.

**Why this matters**  
- Dissociation can feel like being behind glass, watching yourself from outside, losing time, or going blank.  
- It kept you alive during things that were too much to feel.  
- But when dissociation keeps happening in safe situations, it can cost you presence, memory, and connection.

**What it can look like**  
- Feeling numb or flat when you should feel something.  
- Forgetting conversations or appointments.  
- Driving somewhere and not remembering the trip.  
- Feeling like the world is foggy or dreamlike.

**Remember**  
You do not have to hate your dissociation. You just need to learn to come back when it is safe — one small sensation at a time.

---

### Tool for Session 10: The Grounding Check

When you feel foggy, far away, or disconnected:

1. **Name it:** “I am dissociating. My mind is leaving because something feels like too much.”  
2. **Anchor to your body:** Find three sensations — feet on floor, texture of your shirt, temperature of the air.  
3. **Anchor to the room:** Find five things you can see. Three sounds. One thing you can touch.

**Key sentence to carry this week:**  
> *“I can come back. One sensation at a time.”*
