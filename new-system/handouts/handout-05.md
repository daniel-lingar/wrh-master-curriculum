# Handout — Session 05
## Work Became the Place You Could Function

**Big idea:**  
For many people with trauma histories, work becomes an anchor — the only place where they feel competent, useful, and in control. That can save you. But if work is your only anchor, you are one layoff or burnout away from free fall.

**Why this matters**  
- Work gives you predictability, role clarity, and external validation — things your nervous system craves.  
- But when you use work to outrun grief, shame, or chaos at home, you may be working yourself into collapse.  
- Burnout is not weakness. It is your body collecting the debt from too much load and not enough recovery.

**What it can look like**  
- Being praised as the best employee while falling apart at home.  
- Feeling anxious or worthless on days off.  
- Staying late, covering shifts, saying yes to everything — until your body breaks.

**Remember**  
You are more than your output. You deserve to function in more than one room.

---

### Tool for Session 05: The Anchor Inventory

Ask yourself:

1. **What are my anchors?** (Work counts. What else? A person? A hobby? A support group? A walk?)  
2. **If I lost my primary work anchor tomorrow, how many anchors would I have left?**  
3. **What is one small anchor I can begin building this week that has nothing to do with productivity?**

**Key sentence to carry this week:**  
> *“If work is my only anchor, I am one wave away from being lost at sea.”*
