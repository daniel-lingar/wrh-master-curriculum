# Handout — Session 12
## Two Survival Systems Can Fight Inside One Person

**Big idea:**  
You can honestly want two opposite things at the same time. That does not make you crazy, two-faced, or a liar. It means you have more than one survival system inside you.

**Why this matters**  
- Your nervous system learned different ways to survive in different rooms: home, school, relationships, addiction, work.  
- Each “part” or “side” of you remembers what kept you safe back then, and it still tries to protect you now.  
- Those parts can pull in different directions: one part wants sobriety; another wants relief. One wants closeness; another wants to run.

**What it can look like**  
- Making a promise in the morning and breaking it by the afternoon.  
- Saying “I love you” while feeling numb, or feeling everything and saying nothing.  
- Being motivated and hopeful one day, collapsed and hopeless the next.

**Remember**  
Every human being has different “sides” inside; trauma often makes those sides louder and more intense. None of your parts are evil. Each one is trying to do a job — even if that job no longer fits your life.

---

### Tool for Session 12: The Parts Check

Use this anytime you feel torn, confused, or like you’re “self-sabotaging.”

**Step 1 — Name the parts.**  
Silently say: “A part of me wants ______.” “Another part of me wants ______.” No judging. Just notice.

**Step 2 — Ask what each part is protecting.**  
For each part, ask: “What are you trying to protect me from? What are you afraid would happen if you didn’t do your job?”

**Step 3 — Choose consciously.**  
Ask yourself: “What choice honors both parts as much as possible? If I can’t honor both, whose concern is more urgent right now?” Then make a choice. Not because the other part is stupid or bad, but because you are the one who has to live with the consequences.

**Key sentence to carry this week:**  
> *“A part of me wants this. Another part wants that. Both are trying to protect me. Now I choose.”*
