# Handout — Session 01
## The Body Learns Survival Before the Mind Has Words

**Big idea:**  
Your body started learning how to survive before you could talk, before you could understand why you felt afraid, and before you could choose how to react. That is not a flaw. It is how the nervous system works.

**Why this matters**  
- If you grew up with danger, unpredictability, or pain, your body adapted to stay safe.  
- Those adaptations (freezing, pleasing, running, fighting, shutting down) are not choices — they are survival reflexes.  
- The problem is, your body keeps using those reflexes even after the danger is gone.

**What it can look like**  
- Freezing during a simple phone call or before a court date.  
- Feeling terror in a grocery store or a crowded room for no obvious reason.  
- Knowing what you need to do but being completely unable to move or speak.

**Remember**  
Your reactions are not random. They are survival programs your body installed to keep you alive. Now you can learn to read the program.

---

### Tool for Session 01: The Adaptation Check

Ask yourself three questions when your reaction feels bigger than the moment:

1. **What reaction am I having right now?** (Freeze? Run? Fight? Please? Shut down?)  
2. **What environment would this reaction have made sense in?** (What did my body learn to survive?)  
3. **Is this reaction still protecting me, or is it costing me now?**

**Key sentence to carry this week:**  
> *“My body learned to survive. Now I am learning to read the map.”*
