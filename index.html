Scope Boundary
NOT THERAPY. NO DISCLOSURE. WRH is a non-clinical education framework focused on pattern recognition, system logic, nervous-system adaptation, and practical language-building. It does not require or encourage participants to disclose personal trauma history.

Curriculum Architecture

Four-part structure for review, delivery, and fidelity.

Part I

The System Logic Map

Sessions 1–26. Foundational survival laws, pattern recognition, nervous-system adaptation, and plain-language mechanism framing.
Part II

The Core Operational Journey

Sessions 27–56. Core pilot sequence for mechanical loops, wiring patterns, biological programs, regulation tools, and behavioral interruption.
Part III

Advanced System Specialization

Sessions 57–75. Advanced pattern analysis, institutional misreadings, relapse/collapse logic, accountability, and long-term support structures.
Part IV

Facilitator Standardization Toolkit

Delivery standards, facilitator scripts, glossary, staff tools, participant materials, fidelity tools, and implementation support.

Purpose

Language before disclosure.

WRH teaches observable patterns, mechanism language, interruption points, and safer replacement tools. The goal is not to turn classrooms into therapy rooms. The goal is to give people a language for survival patterns before those patterns are misread as defiance, weakness, noncompliance, or moral failure.
