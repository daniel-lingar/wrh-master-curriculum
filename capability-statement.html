Core Capabilities

Curriculum Development

Design and delivery of comprehensive, evidence-informed educational programs covering trauma literacy, institutional navigation, and resilience building.

Facilitator Training

Training and certification programs for facilitators, including safety protocols, crisis management, and participant support frameworks.

Pilot Program Management

Planning, implementation, and evaluation of bounded pilot programs with measurable outcomes and continuous improvement processes.

Safety & Compliance

Development and implementation of safety protocols, risk management frameworks, and compliance procedures for all program delivery.

Outcomes Measurement

Data collection, analysis, and reporting on program effectiveness, participant outcomes, and continuous improvement metrics.

Accessible Design

Curriculum and program design specifically optimized for high-barrier populations, including limited literacy, technology access, and institutional experience.

Service Offerings

1. WRH Master Curriculum

A comprehensive four-part educational program:

  • Part I: Survival Wiring & Trauma Literacy
  • Part II: Institutional Systems & Reentry
  • Part III: Resilience & Autonomy
  • Part IV: Facilitation & Crisis Management

2. Pilot Program Packages

Customized pilot programs for organizations including:

  • Program design and planning
  • Facilitator recruitment and training
  • Participant recruitment and enrollment
  • Session delivery and support
  • Outcomes measurement and reporting
  • Continuous improvement and iteration

3. Facilitator Certification

Comprehensive training and certification for program facilitators, including:

  • Curriculum mastery
  • Safety protocols and crisis response
  • Participant support and trauma-informed practice
  • Compliance and risk management
  • Ongoing professional development

4. Consulting & Technical Assistance

Specialized consulting for organizations implementing WRH or similar programs:

  • Program design and architecture
  • Safety protocol development
  • Outcomes measurement framework
  • Staff training and capacity building
  • Compliance and risk management

Target Populations

WRH services are specifically designed for:

  • Reentry populations (formerly incarcerated individuals)
  • Diversion programs (alternatives to incarceration)
  • Recovery support programs (substance use, behavioral health)
  • Veterans and military-connected populations
  • High-barrier rooms and underserved communities
  • Individuals with limited literacy or technology access
  • Trauma-affected populations seeking education and support

Key Differentiators

Non-Clinical Focus

Educational, not therapeutic. Designed to complement, not replace, clinical mental health services.

Safety-First Approach

Comprehensive safety protocols, trained facilitators, and crisis escalation procedures protect participant welfare.

Evidence-Informed

Grounded in research on trauma, survival responses, and behavioral change, while remaining practical and accessible.

Measurable Outcomes

Pilot programs include rigorous metrics tracking, participant feedback, and data-driven continuous improvement.

Accessible Design

Intentionally designed for high-barrier populations with limited literacy, technology access, or institutional experience.

Comprehensive Support

From curriculum development through facilitator training to ongoing technical assistance and outcomes measurement.
