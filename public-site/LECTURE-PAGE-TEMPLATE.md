# Public Lecture Page Template

Purpose: standardize public-facing WRH lecture pages so they remain clear, non-clinical, low-pressure, and easy to navigate.

This template is adapted from the useful structure identified during the `cptsd-education-portal` consolidation pass.

## Template

```markdown
# [Lecture Title]

## One-Sentence Purpose

State what this lecture helps the reader understand in one plain sentence.

## Plain-Language Overview

Explain the concept without clinical overload. Keep the focus on observable patterns, mechanisms, and safer language.

## The Pattern

What sequence does this lecture help the reader notice?

Use a simple before -> during -> after structure when possible.

## Common Misreading

What label or misunderstanding is often placed on this pattern?

Examples:

- lazy
- manipulative
- dramatic
- noncompliant
- selfish
- weak
- attention-seeking

## WRH Mechanism Translation

Translate the misreading into a mechanism.

Example structure:

> They called it [label]. WRH asks what function the behavior was serving.

## What This Does Not Mean

Clarify boundaries.

This section prevents the lecture from sounding like an excuse, diagnosis, or blanket claim.

Examples:

- This does not mean harm is acceptable.
- This does not remove responsibility.
- This does not diagnose anyone.
- This does not require sharing personal history.

## Reflection Question

Ask one private recognition question.

The question should invite noticing, not disclosure.

Example:

> Where have you seen this pattern show up without having the language for it?

## Optional Discussion Question

Use only for facilitated groups.

Example:

> What changes when a behavior is understood by function instead of label?

## Facilitator Note

Give the facilitator one practical caution.

Example:

> Do not ask participants to explain personal examples. Keep the focus on the pattern.

## Carry-Forward Line

One sentence the reader can remember.

Example:

> Seeing the pattern is not the same as excusing the harm; it is the first step toward interrupting the loop.

## Next Lecture

Link to the next lecture in sequence.
```

## Style Rules

- Use plain language.
- Avoid unnecessary clinical jargon.
- Do not make treatment claims.
- Do not require disclosure.
- Do not overpromise outcomes.
- Do not shame the reader.
- Keep responsibility intact.
- Translate behavior by function, not identity.

## Public Scope Boundary

Each lecture page should make clear that WRH is non-clinical education, not therapy, medical advice, legal advice, crisis care, or a replacement for licensed professionals.
