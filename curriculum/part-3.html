Part III: Resilience & Autonomy

Building agency, autonomy restoration, and integrated behavioral-institutional systems models.

Part III brings it all together. This section integrates learning from Parts I and II to build resilience, restore autonomy, and create sustainable change.

Overview

Part III focuses on building resilience and restoring autonomy. You will learn how to integrate the survival literacy from Part I with the institutional navigation skills from Part II to create sustainable change in your life. This part emphasizes agency, self-determination, and the connection between behavioral and institutional systems.

Duration

4-5 weeks

Approximately 4-5 sessions of 2-3 hours each

Format

Interactive Sessions

Reflection, planning, peer support, and action-oriented activities

Prerequisites

Parts I & II Completion

Understanding of trauma literacy and institutional systems

Core Topics

Session 1: Understanding Resilience

Key Concepts:

  • What is resilience? (not just "bouncing back")
  • Resilience as a systems outcome
  • Individual, relational, and community resilience
  • Building resilience through integrated approaches

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand resilience as a dynamic process
  • Recognize resilience factors in your life
  • Understand how systems support or hinder resilience

Session 2: Autonomy Restoration & Agency Development

Key Concepts:

  • What is autonomy and why it matters
  • How trauma and systems limit autonomy
  • Steps to restore autonomy and agency
  • Making choices and taking action

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand how autonomy has been limited in your life
  • Identify areas where you can restore agency
  • Develop skills for making and implementing choices

Session 3: Identity & Reclassification

Key Concepts:

  • How identity is shaped by experience and systems
  • Moving beyond limiting identities
  • Reclassification and reintegration
  • Creating a new narrative

Learning Objectives:

  • Examine how your identity has been shaped
  • Identify limiting beliefs or identities
  • Develop a more expansive sense of self

Session 4: Integrated Systems & Sustainable Change

Key Concepts:

  • Integrated behavioral-institutional systems models
  • How behavior and systems interact
  • Creating sustainable change at multiple levels
  • Building support and accountability

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand how behavior and systems interact
  • Identify leverage points for change
  • Create a sustainable action plan

Session 5 (Optional): Integration, Celebration & Looking Forward

Key Concepts:

  • Integrating all three parts of the curriculum
  • Celebrating progress and growth
  • Peer support and community connection
  • Continuing your journey beyond WRH

Learning Objectives:

  • Consolidate learning from all three parts
  • Celebrate your growth and progress
  • Plan for continued growth and support

Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of Part III, participants will be able to:

  • Define resilience and understand it as a systems outcome
  • Identify resilience factors in their own lives
  • Understand how autonomy has been limited and how to restore it
  • Develop and exercise agency in their lives
  • Examine and reclassify limiting identities
  • Understand how behavior and systems interact
  • Create and implement sustainable action plans
  • Build and maintain peer support and community connections
  • Integrate learning from all three parts of the curriculum

Key Concepts & Terminology

Term Definition
Resilience The capacity to adapt, recover, and grow in the face of adversity
Agency The capacity and power to act and make choices
Autonomy Self-governance and the ability to make decisions about one's own life
Identity How you understand and describe yourself
Reclassification Changing how you categorize or understand yourself or your situation
Integrated Systems Understanding how individual behavior and institutional systems interact and influence each other
Sustainable Change Change that lasts and is supported by multiple factors (individual, relational, and systemic)

Resources & Further Learning

To deepen your understanding of Part III concepts:

  • Peer Support: Connect with others who have completed WRH or similar programs
  • Ongoing Learning: Continued education and skill-building opportunities
  • Community Connection: See our Resources page for community and peer support organizations

Important Reminders

Your Journey Continues: WRH is a beginning, not an ending. Your growth and resilience building continues beyond this curriculum.

Celebrate Your Progress: Recognize and celebrate the growth, learning, and changes you've made through this curriculum.
