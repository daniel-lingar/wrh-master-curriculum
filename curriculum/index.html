WRH Master Curriculum

The complete four-part curriculum covering survival literacy, institutional systems, resilience, and facilitation.

Welcome to the WRH Master Curriculum. This comprehensive program is designed to support individuals and organizations in understanding trauma, building resilience, and navigating complex systems.

Curriculum Overview

The WRH Master Curriculum is organized into four comprehensive parts, each building on the previous to create a complete educational experience:

Part I

Survival Wiring & Trauma Literacy

Foundational understanding of how the brain and body respond to threat, trauma, and survival.

Detailed Curriculum Structure

Part I: Survival Wiring & Trauma Literacy

Duration: 4-5 weeks | Sessions: 4-5 | Focus: Foundational understanding

Topics Covered:

  • Introduction to survival responses and the nervous system
  • Understanding threat detection and survival wiring
  • Freeze responses and dissociation
  • Trauma-informed frameworks and terminology
  • Recognition of survival responses in daily life
  • Self-awareness and personal reflection

Learning Outcomes:

Participants will understand:

  • How the brain and body respond to threat
  • The difference between normal stress responses and trauma responses
  • How to recognize their own survival responses
  • Basic trauma-informed concepts and language

View Part I

Part II: Institutional Systems & Reentry

Duration: 4-5 weeks | Sessions: 4-5 | Focus: System navigation

Topics Covered:

  • Understanding institutional systems and their impact
  • Reentry pathways and resources
  • Government agencies and social services
  • Healthcare and mental health systems
  • Employment and housing resources
  • Advocacy and self-representation
  • Building support networks

Learning Outcomes:

Participants will be able to:

  • Navigate complex institutional systems
  • Identify and access available resources
  • Advocate for themselves in institutional settings
  • Build and maintain support networks

View Part II

Part III: Resilience & Autonomy

Duration: 4-5 weeks | Sessions: 4-5 | Focus: Building agency

Topics Covered:

  • Autonomy restoration and agency development
  • Resilience as a systems outcome
  • Identity reclassification and reintegration
  • Integrated behavioral-institutional systems models
  • Building sustainable change
  • Peer support and community connection

Learning Outcomes:

Participants will:

  • Develop greater autonomy and agency
  • Build resilience through integrated approaches
  • Create sustainable plans for continued growth
  • Connect with peer support and community

View Part III

Part IV: Facilitation & Crisis Management

Duration: 4-6 weeks | Sessions: 4-6 | Focus: Facilitator training

Topics Covered:

  • Facilitator prerequisites and qualifications
  • Comprehensive facilitator toolkit
  • Safety protocols and crisis response
  • Adverse event reporting and risk management
  • Compliance and legal considerations
  • Certification paths and requirements
  • Participant materials and resources
  • Staff tools and administrative procedures

Learning Outcomes:

Facilitators will:

  • Master all curriculum content
  • Implement safety protocols effectively
  • Respond appropriately to crises
  • Maintain compliance and documentation
  • Achieve certification

View Part IV

Key Principles Throughout

Evidence-Informed

All curriculum content is grounded in research on trauma, survival responses, and behavioral change.

Trauma-Informed

Curriculum is designed with understanding of trauma's impact and principles of safety, trustworthiness, and empowerment.

Accessible

Content is designed for diverse literacy levels and learning styles, with accommodations available.

Practical

Curriculum includes concrete skills, strategies, and resources applicable to real-world situations.

Participatory

Sessions include interactive activities, group discussion, and participant reflection.

Safe

Clear protocols, trained facilitators, and crisis procedures ensure participant safety throughout.
