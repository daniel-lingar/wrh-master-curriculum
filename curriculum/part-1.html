Part I: Survival Wiring & Trauma Literacy

Foundational understanding of how the brain and body respond to threat, trauma, and survival.

Part I is the foundation of WRH. This section builds your understanding of survival responses, trauma, and the nervous system. All subsequent parts build on these foundational concepts.

Overview

Part I introduces the core concepts and frameworks that underpin the entire WRH curriculum. You will learn about how the brain and body respond to threat, understand different survival responses, and develop a trauma-informed perspective on human behavior and resilience.

Duration

4-5 weeks

Approximately 4-5 sessions of 2-3 hours each

Format

Interactive Sessions

Lecture, discussion, activities, and reflection

Prerequisites

None

Open to all. No prior experience or education required.

Core Topics

Session 1: Introduction to Survival Responses

Key Concepts:

  • The nervous system and threat detection
  • Fight, flight, and freeze responses
  • Survival wiring and evolutionary perspective
  • Normal stress vs. traumatic stress

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand how the nervous system detects and responds to threat
  • Recognize different survival responses
  • Understand the evolutionary basis of survival wiring

Session 2: Understanding Freeze & Dissociation

Key Concepts:

  • The freeze response and immobilization
  • Dissociation and disconnection
  • How freeze protects in the moment
  • Long-term impacts of chronic freeze

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand the freeze response and its function
  • Recognize signs of dissociation
  • Understand how freeze can become problematic

Session 3: Trauma-Informed Frameworks

Key Concepts:

  • What is trauma? (definition and types)
  • Single-incident vs. complex/chronic trauma
  • Trauma's impact on brain, body, and behavior
  • Trauma-informed principles and language

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand different types of trauma
  • Recognize trauma's effects on the whole person
  • Learn trauma-informed language and perspective

Session 4: Recognizing Your Own Responses

Key Concepts:

  • Personal survival response patterns
  • Triggers and activation
  • How past experiences shape current responses
  • Self-awareness and reflection

Learning Objectives:

  • Identify your own survival response patterns
  • Recognize personal triggers
  • Develop self-awareness about your nervous system responses

Session 5 (Optional): Integration & Reflection

Key Concepts:

  • Integrating Part I learning
  • Applying concepts to real-world situations
  • Questions and clarification
  • Preparing for Part II

Learning Objectives:

  • Consolidate learning from Part I
  • Apply concepts to personal and community contexts
  • Prepare for Part II: Institutional Systems & Reentry

Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of Part I, participants will be able to:

  • Explain how the nervous system responds to threat and stress
  • Describe different survival responses (fight, flight, freeze)
  • Understand the distinction between normal stress and traumatic stress
  • Recognize signs of dissociation and freeze in themselves and others
  • Define trauma and understand its impacts
  • Apply trauma-informed language and perspective
  • Identify their own survival response patterns and triggers
  • Understand how past experiences shape current nervous system responses

Key Concepts & Terminology

Term Definition
Nervous System The body's communication network that detects threat and coordinates survival responses
Threat Detection The process by which the nervous system identifies potential danger
Survival Response Automatic reactions (fight, flight, freeze) to perceived threat
Freeze Response Immobilization or shutdown in response to inescapable threat
Dissociation Disconnection from thoughts, feelings, body, or surroundings
Trauma Exposure to actual or threatened death, serious injury, or sexual violence
Trigger A stimulus that activates a trauma response or survival reaction
Trauma-Informed Approach that recognizes trauma's widespread impact and integrates knowledge about trauma into practice

Resources & Further Learning

To deepen your understanding of Part I concepts:

  • Recommended Reading: "The Body Keeps the Score" by Bessel van der Kolk
  • Video Resources: TED talks on trauma and the nervous system
  • Online Courses: Free courses on trauma-informed care and neurobiology
  • Support Resources: See our Resources page for crisis support and mental health services

Important Reminders

WRH is educational, not clinical. If you experience strong emotional reactions or need professional support, please reach out to a mental health provider or crisis line.

Confidentiality & Safety: All participant information is kept confidential. Sessions include clear safety protocols and crisis procedures.
