Part IV: Facilitation & Crisis Management

Facilitator toolkit, safety protocols, certification paths, and crisis response frameworks.

Part IV is for Facilitators. This section provides the tools, protocols, and training needed to safely and effectively deliver WRH curriculum. It is essential for ensuring participant safety and program quality.

Overview

Part IV is designed for individuals who will be facilitating WRH curriculum. It covers the facilitator's role, safety protocols, crisis management, and the administrative aspects of program delivery. Completion of Part IV, along with Parts I-III, is required for facilitator certification.

Duration

4-6 weeks

Approximately 4-6 sessions of 2-3 hours each

Format

Training & Practicum

Instruction, role-play, safety drills, and supervised facilitation

Prerequisites

Parts I-III Completion

Mastery of curriculum content and trauma-informed principles

Core Topics

Session 1: The Facilitator's Role & Prerequisites

Key Concepts:

  • Facilitator qualifications and prerequisites
  • Role boundaries: Educator vs. Therapist
  • Trauma-informed facilitation principles
  • Self-care and vicarious trauma prevention

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand the facilitator's role and responsibilities
  • Maintain appropriate professional boundaries
  • Implement self-care strategies to prevent burnout

Session 2: Safety Protocols & Crisis Response

Key Concepts:

  • WRH Safety Protocols (comprehensive review)
  • Identifying signs of crisis or distress
  • Crisis escalation procedures
  • Emergency response and clinical referrals
  • Safety drills and practice

Learning Objectives:

  • Master all WRH safety protocols
  • Respond appropriately to participant distress or crisis
  • Implement escalation and referral procedures effectively

Session 3: Facilitator Toolkit & Materials

Key Concepts:

  • Session guides and facilitator notes
  • Participant workbooks and handouts
  • Interactive activities and exercises
  • Managing group dynamics and difficult conversations

Learning Objectives:

  • Use facilitator materials effectively to deliver curriculum
  • Facilitate interactive activities and discussions
  • Manage group dynamics in a trauma-informed way

Session 4: Adverse Event Reporting & Risk Management

Key Concepts:

  • What is an adverse event?
  • Reporting procedures and documentation
  • Risk management frameworks
  • Compliance and legal considerations

Learning Objectives:

  • Identify and report adverse events correctly
  • Maintain accurate documentation and records
  • Understand and follow risk management protocols

Session 5: Certification & Administrative Tools

Key Concepts:

  • Certification path and requirements
  • Staff tools and administrative procedures
  • Data collection and outcomes measurement
  • Continuous professional development

Learning Objectives:

  • Complete all certification requirements
  • Use administrative tools for program management
  • Collect and report data on program outcomes

Facilitator Certification Path

To become a certified WRH facilitator, candidates must:

  1. Complete Parts I-III: Demonstrate mastery of curriculum content
  2. Complete Part IV: Successfully complete facilitator training
  3. Pass Safety Assessment: Demonstrate competence in safety protocols and crisis response
  4. Supervised Practicum: Facilitate sessions under the supervision of a certified trainer
  5. Final Review: Receive positive evaluation and recommendation for certification

Safety & Crisis Management Framework

Tiered Response System

Level Description Action Required
Level 1: Distress Participant shows signs of emotional distress or activation Facilitator uses de-escalation; participant takes break; check-in after session
Level 2: Escalation Participant's distress increases; potential for disruption or harm Facilitator follows escalation protocol; potential clinical referral; documentation
Level 3: Crisis Immediate threat of harm to self or others; medical emergency Facilitator contacts emergency services; immediate clinical referral; reporting

Important Reminders for Facilitators

Safety is Priority #1: Never compromise safety for curriculum delivery. Follow all protocols and escalate concerns immediately.

Maintain Boundaries: You are an educator, not a therapist. Refer participants to clinical services for treatment or diagnosis.

Self-Care: Facilitating trauma-informed curriculum can be demanding. Prioritize your own well-being and seek support when needed.
