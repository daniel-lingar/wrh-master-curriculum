# Pre/Post Belief-Shift Survey

Source: harvested and cleaned from the legacy `What-Really-Happened-Manual` short-form facilitator version.

Purpose: provide a simple before/after reflection measure for non-clinical education settings.

## Use Boundary

This is not a diagnostic instrument.

This is not a clinical assessment.

This is not a mental-health screening tool.

It is a plain-language educational reflection tool designed to help facilitators and participants notice whether understanding, shame, self-blame, and perceived tool access shifted during the program.

## Instructions

Ask participants to rate each statement from 1 to 5.

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Strongly disagree |
| 2 | Disagree |
| 3 | Not sure / neutral |
| 4 | Agree |
| 5 | Strongly agree |

## Survey Items

1. I understand that survival patterns can form for understandable reasons.
2. I can name at least one pattern my nervous system uses under stress.
3. I feel less shame about past coping patterns than I did before.
4. I know at least one tool I can use when I feel overwhelmed.
5. I understand the difference between responsibility and shame.
6. I can pause and notice a pattern before judging myself.
7. I believe change is possible without pretending the past did not happen.

## Facilitator Notes

Use this survey to track learning, not to grade participants.

Avoid pressuring participants to explain their answers.

If used in a pilot or institutional setting, keep responses anonymous unless the program has a clear consent process and data-use explanation.

## Interpretation Boundary

A score change may suggest a shift in language, recognition, or confidence. It does not prove treatment effect, clinical improvement, or behavioral outcome by itself.
