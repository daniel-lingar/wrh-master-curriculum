# Plan B Handout

Source: harvested and cleaned from the legacy `What-Really-Happened-Manual` short-form facilitator version.

Purpose: give participants a simple, non-disruptive safety plan for moments when the material feels heavy.

## My Plan B: When the Material Gets Heavy

You do not have to perform, explain, confess, or push through activation.

You are allowed to slow down.

You are allowed to step out.

You are allowed to pass.

## Yellow Light

Use this when you feel mildly activated, tense, restless, distracted, ashamed, angry, sad, or overwhelmed but still present.

Try one of these:

- press both feet into the floor
- notice the chair holding you
- name five objects in the room
- take one slow breath out
- look around and remind yourself where you are
- put one hand on your leg or the table to feel contact

Suggested words:

> I am here. This is now. I do not have to solve everything in this moment.

## Red Light

Use this when you feel flooded, shut down, panicked, frozen, too exposed, or unable to stay present.

You may:

- step out without asking
- get water
- walk briefly
- sit somewhere quieter
- return when ready
- stay silent when you return

You do not owe the room an explanation.

## If the Room Gets Heavy

The facilitator may pause the session and invite the group to reset.

This is not failure. It is part of the design.

## What This Tool Is For

Plan B is not therapy. It is a simple support tool for staying oriented during educational material.

The goal is not to force emotion down. The goal is to keep enough safety and choice in the room that learning can continue.
