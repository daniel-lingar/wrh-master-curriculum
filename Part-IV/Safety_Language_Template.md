## **Purpose**
This template provides the **Hardened Safety Language** required for all 76 sessions of the WRH Master Curriculum. This language is non-negotiable and must be used to maintain federal compliance and participant safety.

---

## **1.0 The Universal Opening (The Anchor)**
*To be used at the start of every session.*

> "We are entering a high-activation space. You are the operator of your own machine. At any point, you may use the **Yellow Light** (pause/slow down) or **Red Light** (stop/exit). We will begin with the **Regulation Grounding Protocol (RGP)**. Feet flat, eyes open or closed, breathe in for four... hold... out for six."

---

## **2.0 The Non-Clinical Disclaimer**
*To be used when participants share deep personal trauma.*

> "I hear you, and I see the system logic in that response. To keep this space safe and compliant, we are focusing on the **mechanical wiring** of the response, not the clinical processing of the event. If you feel you are drifting into a 'Red Light' zone, let's return to the RGP together."

---

## **3.0 The Crisis Warm-Handoff**
*To be used if a participant indicates acute distress or risk of self-harm.*

> "The system is showing high activation right now. My role is to provide the curriculum logic; the clinical safety team is here to provide the support. We are going to transition to the **Safety Planning Intervention (SPI)** protocol now. [Insert name of clinical lead] will take over from here. You are safe, and the system is working."

---

## **4.0 The Universal Closing (The Shift)**
*To be used at the end of every session.*

> "The session is closing, but the machine is still running. Your tool for the next 24 hours is [Insert Session Tool]. Remember: You are not the broken parts; you are the leader of the team. Yellow or red anytime. You're free."
