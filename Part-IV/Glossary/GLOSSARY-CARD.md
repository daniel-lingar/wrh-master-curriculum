# WRH Glossary Card

Source: harvested and cleaned from the legacy `What-Really-Happened-Manual` short-form facilitator version.

Purpose: provide a short participant-facing vocabulary card for the WRH curriculum.

## Use Boundary

These are plain-language educational definitions.

They are not diagnostic labels and should not be used to diagnose, shame, or reduce a person to a category.

## Core Terms

### CPTSD

Complex trauma patterns that can form when danger, instability, neglect, abuse, or emotional unsafety repeat over time.

Plain-language frame:

> The system adapted to danger that lasted too long.

### Fawn Response

A survival pattern where a person tries to stay safe by pleasing, appeasing, over-explaining, overworking, or becoming useful.

Plain-language frame:

> Safety through approval.

### Freeze Response

A survival pattern where the body slows down, locks up, goes quiet, or cannot move into action even when the person wants to.

Plain-language frame:

> The system hit overload and protected itself by shutting down.

### Gaslighting

A pattern where someone repeatedly denies, distorts, or reverses reality until another person starts doubting what they saw, felt, or remember.

Plain-language frame:

> Reality gets argued with until the target stops trusting their own mind.

### Trauma Bond

A strong attachment that forms through cycles of fear, relief, hurt, repair, hope, and repeated emotional intensity.

Plain-language frame:

> The nervous system mistakes the cycle for connection.

### Love Bombing

Overwhelming attention, affection, promises, or intensity that creates fast attachment before trust has had time to form.

Plain-language frame:

> Too much closeness too fast.

### Amygdala Alarm

The brain/body alarm system that reacts to perceived danger.

Plain-language frame:

> The smoke detector goes off before the thinking brain catches up.

### Pattern Recognition

The ability to notice a repeated sequence before it fully takes over.

Plain-language frame:

> Seeing the loop early enough to interrupt it.

### Self-Attunement

The practice of noticing what the body, mind, and emotions are signaling without immediately judging or ignoring them.

Plain-language frame:

> Listening before reacting.

### Regulation

The process of helping the body return toward steadiness after stress, activation, shutdown, or overwhelm.

Plain-language frame:

> Helping the system come back online.

## Reminder

The goal is not to collect labels.

The goal is to recognize patterns, name mechanisms, and build safer choices.
