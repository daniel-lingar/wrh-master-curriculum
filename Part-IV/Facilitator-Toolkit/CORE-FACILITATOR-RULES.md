# Core Facilitator Rules

Source: harvested and cleaned from the legacy `What-Really-Happened-Manual` short-form facilitator version.

Purpose: give facilitators simple delivery rules that support safety, pacing, clarity, and non-clinical containment.

## Primary Delivery Rules

| Rule | Practice |
|---|---|
| **Pause** | After a key reframe or important line, pause long enough for the room to absorb it. |
| **Drop** | Lower the voice slightly on the second half of important sentences. This prevents the delivery from feeling rushed or performative. |
| **Breathe** | Take one slow breath before moving into rapid reframes or emotionally heavy material. |
| **Single-point attention** | When asking a mirror question, speak gently to the room without scanning aggressively or pressuring anyone to respond. |

## Room Safety Rules

### Yellow Light

A participant appears mildly activated, restless, overwhelmed, shut down, or emotionally stirred.

Facilitator response:

- pause the teaching
- slow the room down
- invite feet on the floor
- take one slow breath together
- continue only after the room settles

### Red Light

A participant appears flooded or needs to leave.

Facilitator response:

- allow exit without explanation
- do not chase, interrogate, or demand disclosure
- keep the room calm
- return to grounding before continuing

### Heavy Room

If the whole room feels heavy, stop teaching briefly.

Suggested facilitator language:

> Let's pause for a moment. Feet on the floor. One slow breath out. We are going to let the room settle before we continue.

## Facilitator Self-Regulation

If the facilitator becomes activated:

- pause
- say, "I need a moment."
- take a few slow breaths
- model regulation instead of pretending nothing happened

A regulated facilitator is part of the curriculum container.

## Back-to-Back Session Limit

Do not schedule more than two emotionally heavy WRH sessions back-to-back without a reset period.

The facilitator's nervous system matters. Burned-out delivery weakens fidelity and increases risk.

## After-Session Decompression

After each session, take five minutes before returning to normal tasks.

Suggested process:

1. Write down one thing noticed about the room.
2. Do not try to fix or interpret everything.
3. Shake out hands or move the body briefly.
4. Drink water.
5. Leave the session inside the container.

Core reminder:

> You are not responsible for forcing anyone's healing. You are responsible for protecting the container.

## Non-Clinical Boundary

Facilitators do not diagnose, treat, investigate, interrogate, or require disclosure.

The job is to:

- teach the pattern
- name the mechanism
- keep the room safe
- redirect toward safer tools
- refer out when licensed care, crisis care, or mandated reporting is required
