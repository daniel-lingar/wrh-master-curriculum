## **Overview**
To ensure the high-fidelity delivery of the **"What Really Happened" (WRH) Master Curriculum**, facilitators must meet specific prerequisite standards. These requirements scale across all 76 sessions and are divided into three professional tiers.

---

## **1.0 Prerequisite Matrix**

| Tier | Designation | Authorized Scope | Prerequisites |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tier 1** | **Foundational Facilitator** | Sessions 1–26 | - 40 hours of WRH System Logic Training<br>- Proficiency in RGP (Regulation Grounding Protocol)<br>- Background in Peer Support or Behavioral Health |
| **Tier 2** | **Core Facilitator** | Sessions 1–56 | - Tier 1 Certification + 60 additional hours<br>- Successful delivery of one full Foundation Cohort<br>- Advanced Somatic Redline Recognition Training |
| **Tier 3** | **Master Facilitator** | Sessions 1–75+ | - Tier 2 Certification + 100 additional hours<br>- Institutional Risk Management Certification<br>- Authorization to train other facilitators |

---

## **2.0 Core Competencies (All Tiers)**
Regardless of tier, all facilitators must demonstrate mastery in the following "Hardened" competencies:

### **2.1 System Logic Translation**
The ability to translate complex trauma responses into engineering and mechanical metaphors without losing the human element.

### **2.2 Safety Protocol Adherence**
Strict adherence to the [**Standardized Safety Language Template**](./Safety_Language_Template.md) and the ability to execute a "Warm Handoff" to clinical staff.

### **2.3 Script Fidelity**
Maintaining the integrity of the **Core Script** while managing room energy. Facilitators must understand that the script is a "Hardened" asset designed for safety.

---

## **3.0 Certification Maintenance**
*   **Annual Audit**: Facilitators must undergo one live session audit per year by a Tier 3 Master Facilitator.
*   **Continuing Education**: 10 hours of updated Federal Compliance and Risk Management training annually.
*   **Ethics Review**: Adherence to the Capitol Contracts LLC Facilitator Code of Conduct.
