Start Here: WRH Pilot Overview

Welcome to WRH Master Curriculum — Non-clinical survival-mode education with safety boundaries and measurable pilot delivery.

What is WRH?

WRH is structured, non-clinical education designed for individuals navigating trauma, institutional systems, and recovery. It is specifically designed for:

  • Reentry populations (formerly incarcerated individuals)
  • Diversion programs (alternatives to incarceration)
  • Recovery support (substance use, behavioral health)
  • Veterans and military-connected populations
  • High-barrier rooms and underserved communities

What WRH Is NOT

It is critical to understand what WRH does not provide:

  • Not therapy: WRH is educational, not therapeutic treatment
  • Not diagnosis: We do not diagnose mental health conditions
  • Not treatment: WRH does not replace clinical services
  • Not medical: We do not provide medical or psychiatric intervention

If you are experiencing a mental health crisis or need clinical support, please contact a qualified mental health professional or crisis line.

Core Principles

Safety First

Clear protocols, trained facilitators, and crisis escalation procedures protect participant safety throughout all sessions.

Evidence-Informed

Curriculum is grounded in research on trauma, survival responses, and behavioral change.

Measurable Outcomes

Pilot programs track metrics, participant feedback, and continuous improvement.

Accessible Design

Designed for high-barrier populations with limited literacy, technology access, or institutional experience.

The Four-Part Curriculum

WRH Master Curriculum is organized into four comprehensive parts:

Part I: Survival Wiring & Trauma Literacy

Focus: Understanding how the brain and body respond to threat and trauma.

Topics include:

  • Survival responses and nervous system activation
  • Freeze states and dissociation
  • Trauma-informed frameworks
  • Recognition and self-awareness

Frequently Asked Questions

Is WRH right for me?

WRH is designed for individuals interested in understanding trauma, survival responses, and institutional navigation. It is not a replacement for clinical mental health services. If you are in crisis or experiencing severe mental health symptoms, please seek professional help immediately.

How long does the curriculum take?

The full curriculum can be completed over several weeks to months, depending on your pace and context. Pilot programs typically run 12-16 weeks with weekly sessions.

Do I need prior experience or education?

No. WRH is designed to be accessible to all literacy levels and backgrounds. The curriculum is intentionally designed for high-barrier populations.

What if I'm in crisis?

WRH includes clear crisis protocols and escalation procedures. If you experience a crisis during a session, facilitators are trained to respond appropriately and connect you with emergency services if needed.

Can I participate while working with a therapist?

Yes. WRH is designed to complement, not replace, clinical services. We encourage participants to maintain relationships with their existing providers.
