WRH Pilot Package

A WRH pilot is a bounded agency test before wider rollout. Our pilot packages provide everything needed to implement, evaluate, and scale WRH programming.

What is a Pilot Program?

A pilot program is a limited, time-bounded test of WRH curriculum delivery within your organization. It allows you to:

  • Evaluate program fit and effectiveness in your context
  • Build staff capacity and expertise
  • Gather data on participant outcomes and satisfaction
  • Identify implementation challenges and solutions
  • Make informed decisions about scaling to wider rollout

Pilot Program Structure

A typical WRH pilot program includes:

Duration

12-16 weeks of weekly sessions

Allows time for full curriculum delivery plus evaluation and reflection.

Participant Group

15-25 participants per cohort

Manageable size for facilitator attention and group dynamics.

Facilitators

1-2 trained facilitators per cohort

Includes facilitator training and ongoing technical assistance.

Session Format

Weekly 2-3 hour sessions

In-person or hybrid delivery options available.

Pilot Package Components

1. Program Design & Planning

  • Needs assessment and context analysis
  • Participant recruitment and enrollment strategy
  • Facilitator selection and matching
  • Session scheduling and logistics planning
  • Safety protocols and crisis response procedures
  • Outcomes measurement framework

2. Facilitator Training & Certification

  • Comprehensive curriculum training (all four parts)
  • Trauma-informed facilitation skills
  • Safety protocols and crisis management
  • Participant support and de-escalation techniques
  • Data collection and outcomes measurement
  • Ongoing supervision and technical assistance
  • Certification upon successful completion

3. Curriculum Materials & Resources

  • Complete WRH Master Curriculum (all four parts)
  • Session guides and facilitator notes
  • Participant workbooks and handouts
  • Safety protocols and crisis response templates
  • Data collection tools and assessment instruments
  • Resource lists and referral guides

4. Participant Support

  • Recruitment and enrollment support
  • Orientation and onboarding
  • Attendance tracking and engagement monitoring
  • Crisis support and escalation procedures
  • Referral to clinical services when needed
  • Post-program follow-up and evaluation

5. Outcomes Measurement & Evaluation

  • Pre- and post-program assessments
  • Session attendance and engagement tracking
  • Participant satisfaction surveys
  • Facilitator observations and notes
  • Data analysis and reporting
  • Continuous improvement recommendations

6. Technical Assistance & Support

  • Ongoing consultation with WRH architects
  • Facilitator supervision and coaching
  • Problem-solving and troubleshooting
  • Adaptation guidance for your context
  • Regular check-ins and progress reviews
  • Scaling recommendations upon completion

Pilot Program Timeline

Phase Timeline Key Activities
Planning & Preparation Weeks 1-4 Needs assessment, facilitator selection, participant recruitment, logistics planning
Facilitator Training Weeks 5-8 Comprehensive curriculum training, safety protocols, facilitation skills, certification
Participant Onboarding Week 9 Participant orientation, informed consent, pre-program assessment, group building
Curriculum Delivery Weeks 10-25 Weekly sessions covering Parts I-IV, attendance tracking, participant support

Success Criteria

A successful WRH pilot program demonstrates:

Participant Engagement

High attendance rates (80%+) and active participation in sessions.

Participant Satisfaction

Positive feedback on curriculum relevance, facilitator quality, and safety.

Facilitator Competence

Facilitators demonstrate mastery of curriculum and effective facilitation skills.

Safety & Compliance

No safety incidents; all protocols followed; appropriate escalations when needed.

Organizational Readiness

Staff capacity built; systems in place; commitment to continued delivery.

Measurable Outcomes

Participants show growth in knowledge, skills, and resilience indicators.

Next Steps After Pilot

Upon successful completion of a pilot program, organizations can:

  • Scale to Multiple Cohorts: Expand to serve more participants with trained facilitators
  • Integrate into Existing Programs: Embed WRH curriculum within current services
  • Train Additional Facilitators: Build internal capacity for ongoing delivery
  • Customize for Your Context: Adapt curriculum based on pilot learnings
  • Establish Sustainability: Develop funding and staffing models for long-term delivery

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a pilot program cost?

Pricing is customized based on your organization's needs, participant numbers, facilitator training, and support level. Contact us for a detailed quote.

How long does a pilot program take?

A typical pilot is 6 months from planning through final evaluation and reporting. The curriculum delivery itself is 12-16 weeks.

Do we need to hire new staff?

Not necessarily. We can train existing staff to be facilitators. However, you'll need to allocate time and resources for facilitator training and curriculum delivery.

What if participants need clinical support?

WRH includes clear protocols for identifying when participants need clinical services and connecting them with appropriate providers. We support this referral process.

Can we customize the curriculum for our population?

Yes. While the core curriculum remains consistent, we can adapt examples, case studies, and delivery methods to fit your specific population and context.

What happens if the pilot is successful?

We provide scaling recommendations and support your organization in expanding to additional cohorts, training more facilitators, and establishing sustainable delivery.
